Analysis of Cloze results

1 Introduction

This page describes the results of a Cloze task, which is a technique in psycholinguistic studies to test for the predictability of words given a specific context (Mirault et al., 2021; Snell & Grainger, 2019). This task was used to help compare the predictability of the target words used in a reading task about the role surprisal has on the pronunciation of t-glottalization and strengthening (Brinkerhoff et al., 2025).

2 Methods

2.1 Sentences

The sentences used in the cloze task were identical to the sentences used in the reading task from Brinkerhoff et al. (2025). The sentences were constructed for thirty target words in two different conditions. The words were selected from three phonological construction: (i) 10 words with word final /tn/ clusters, (ii) 10 words with the FAIL vowel precedinging a word final lateral, e.g., /e͡ɪl/, and (iii) 10 words with the FELL vowel preceding a word final lateral, e.g., /ɛl/. This wors were selected because of their claimed status as dialectal markers in Utahn English. These thirty words were placed in sentences constructed to minipulate the infomation load of the target word. The conditions were high information load and low information load.
